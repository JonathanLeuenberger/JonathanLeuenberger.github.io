About Me

I am an undergraduate researcher at New Mexico State University pursuing Ph.D. positions within the field of Human-Computer Interaction. I am advised by Dr. Shiva Darian, conducting research on how refugees experience and adapt to mobile technologies while seeking asylum at the US–Mexico Border. I am also working with Dr. Bill Hamilton on phishing scams in short-form video platforms.

Broadly, I am interested in community collaborative work and qualitative methods — exploring how technology intersects with culture, migration, and marginalized communities. I am especially drawn to the role of online communities and systems of control in shaping how people experience digital life.

Major: Anthropology  |  Expected Graduation: May 2027
