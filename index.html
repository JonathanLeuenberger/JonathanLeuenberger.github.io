Research

Mobile Technologies and Refugee Experiences at the US–Mexico Borderh
As refugees enter the U.S. via the southern border, they must navigate a complex technological transition during the temporary asylum phase. Despite numerous challenges—including unstable access, surveillance, and privacy concerns—refugees rely heavily on mobile phones for navigation, communication, and accessing critical information. This research examines how refugees experience and adapt to these technologies, the affordances and limitations of mobile device use, and how legal and social systems interact with digital divides inequalities. By exploring refugees’ perceptions of surveillance and their use of online communities, this work aims to shed light on the multifaceted role of technology in shaping migration experiences during a critical and vulnerable period.

Phishing scams on TikTok and Douyin
This project examines phishing strategies on TikTok and Douyin, with the goal of identifying common techniques used by attackers and understanding which types of social engineering tactics users are most susceptible to. The study focuses on platform-specific features, user behavior, and the evolving nature of phishing threats in short-form video-based social media environments.
